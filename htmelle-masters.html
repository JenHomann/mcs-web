    • HTMelle Masters

    continuing web development

    location: Omaha Public Library, Milton R. Abrahams Branch computer lab

    Saturdays, January 12 - March 9

    In this course, students will create their own websites and build on their HTML and CSS skills.


    Applications Open Now

    HTMelle Masters is a 9 week intermediate coding class for students in 7th through 12th grade who know a little about HTML and CSS but want to learn more. Students will build on their HTML and CSS skills, learn a bit about JavaScript and DOM manipulation, and use their newly mastered skills to create their own websites. A home computer is not required to take this class. This class is a continuation of HTMelle, our beginning web development class, in which students learned HTML and CSS fundamentals, but students who picked up their HTML and CSS knowledge elsewhere are welcome to apply.

    Sandi Barr is the instructor for this class, and Ella Hubbard is our volunteer coordinator. We are in need of a few more volunteers to work one-on-one with the students in the classroom to help them overcome any challenges and provide guidance on their projects. Sign up to volunteer here!

    We ask that all of our students adhere to these Classroom Guidelines when participating in our programs.

    *This class is all female students including those who were not assigned female at birth and those who identify as femme, gender fluid, or gender non-conforming.
